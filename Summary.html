Born A Crime

Trevor Noah tells stories about his childhood, and in telling the story.
he gives insight into his country’s history, culture, and traditions.
Trevor's mother was one of the devout Christians who took her family to church.
Every Sunday where they attended different churches every Sunday.
she believed that different churches offered different things.

Trevour enjoyed attending churches even though hated the process of commuting.
To churches especially when their secondhand car was not working.
Where they had to use minibuses which was a very complex means of transport.
I relate to this because as I was growing up I loved going to church.
But anytime started raining I would look for all excuses not to attend.
Trevor was thrown out of a moving bus to protect him and her mother jumped.
After him but Trevor was bleeding on confused and he didn’t know what was happening.
I relate to this most of my parents including my own would go to any extent to ensure we are safe.

The second chapter shows how Trevor grew up during apartheid which was weird because,
he was raised in a mixed family where his father was a white Swiss man and
his mother was black.During this time Trevor's existence was against the law therefore
it was hard for both Trevor's parents to be seen together with their son.
In relation to our county people from different tribes that are not from this
our tribe especially males are viewed as workers such as Shamba boys or for ladies
are viewed as Nany’s or House managers.
